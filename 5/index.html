Case Study 1: Consolidation with Foreign Subsidiary

Advanced Accounting AS-21 Ind AS 110

Sunshine Ltd., an Indian company, acquired 80% of Moonbeam GmbH, a German company, on 1st April 2022 for €5 million when the exchange rate was ₹85/€. The following are the financials as of 31st March 2023:

Particulars Sunshine Ltd. (₹ in crores) Moonbeam GmbH (€ in millions)
Share Capital 200 3
Retained Earnings 75 1.2
Fixed Assets 350 4.5

Additional Information:

  1. Moonbeam's retained earnings on acquisition date were €0.8 million
  2. Exchange rate on 31st March 2023: ₹92/€
  3. Sunshine Ltd. had advanced a loan of ₹15 crores to Moonbeam GmbH
  4. Moonbeam declared dividend of €0.3 million
Questions:
  1. Calculate goodwill/capital reserve on acquisition
  2. Compute exchange difference to be shown in OCI
  3. Prepare consolidated financial statements
  4. Explain the treatment of dividend in consolidation
Note: Consider relevant provisions of Ind AS 21 and Ind AS 110. Show all workings.

Case Study 2: Business Combination with Contingent Consideration

Advanced Accounting Ind AS 103

PQR Ltd. acquired ABC Ltd. on 1st April 2022. The acquisition agreement included:

  • Upfront payment of ₹50 crores
  • Additional payment of ₹5 crores if ABC's EBITDA exceeds ₹20 crores in FY 2022-23
  • Payment of ₹3 crores if certain patents are approved within 2 years

As of acquisition date:

  • Probability of EBITDA target being met: 60%
  • Probability of patent approval: 40%
  • Fair value of net identifiable assets: ₹48 crores

As of 31st March 2023:

  • ABC's actual EBITDA: ₹22 crores
  • Patent approval still pending
Questions:
  1. Calculate goodwill on acquisition date
  2. Determine the accounting treatment as of 31st March 2023
  3. Prepare relevant journal entries for the contingent consideration
  4. Explain the disclosure requirements under Ind AS 103

Case Study 3: Complex Tax Scenario for Corporate Restructuring

Taxation Corporate Restructuring

XYZ Ltd. is undergoing restructuring involving:

  1. Demerger of its manufacturing division to ABC Ltd. (a wholly owned subsidiary)
  2. Transfer of investments to a new entity DEF Ltd. in exchange for shares
  3. Sale of a division to an unrelated party for ₹120 crores (book value ₹80 crores)

Additional Information:

  • XYZ Ltd. has brought forward business loss of ₹25 crores
  • Unabsorbed depreciation: ₹15 crores
  • Capital gains exemption under section 47(vi) is being considered
  • Stamp duty implications for the demerger
Questions:
  1. Analyze the tax implications of each restructuring component
  2. Compute the taxable capital gains, if any
  3. Determine the availability of brought forward losses post-restructuring
  4. Suggest optimal structure to minimize tax liability
