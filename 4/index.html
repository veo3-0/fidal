CA Inter Advanced Accounting - Case Study (Marks: 15)

Topic: Consolidation of Financial Statements & Ind AS Adjustments

Case Study: Merger of Alpha Ltd. & Beta Ltd.

Background: Alpha Ltd. acquired 75% of Beta Ltd. on 1st April 2021. The following are the Balance Sheets of both companies as of 31st March 2023:

Particulars Alpha Ltd. (₹ in Lakhs) Beta Ltd. (₹ in Lakhs)
Equity Share Capital (₹10 each) 500 200
Retained Earnings 120 50
General Reserve 80 30
10% Debentures 200 100
Trade Payables 100 70
Total Liabilities 1,000 450
Fixed Assets 600 300
Investment in Beta Ltd. (75%) 180 -
Inventory 150 100
Trade Receivables 70 50
Total Assets 1,000 450
Additional Information:
  1. On acquisition date, Beta Ltd.’s retained earnings were ₹30 lakhs, and General Reserve was ₹20 lakhs.
  2. Alpha Ltd. sold goods worth ₹50 lakhs to Beta Ltd. at a 25% profit margin. 40% of these goods remain unsold.
  3. Beta Ltd. declared a dividend of ₹20 lakhs, which was not yet paid.
  4. Goodwill impairment of ₹5 lakhs was identified during the year.
Questions:
  1. Calculate the Goodwill or Capital Reserve arising on acquisition.
  2. Compute the Non-Controlling Interest (NCI) as of 31st March 2023.
  3. Adjust for the unrealized profit in inventory.
  4. Prepare the Consolidated Balance Sheet after all adjustments.
Note: Assume a corporate tax rate of 30% where applicable. Show all workings clearly.
