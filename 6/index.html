Case Study 4: Financial Instruments - Compound Instruments

Advanced Accounting Ind AS 32 Ind AS 109

Sunrise Ltd. issued 10,000 convertible debentures on 1st April 2022 with following terms:

  • Face value: ₹1,000 per debenture
  • Coupon rate: 8% payable annually
  • Maturity: 5 years
  • Conversion option: 50 equity shares per debenture at maturity

Market information as on 1st April 2022:

  • Similar non-convertible debentures yield: 10%
  • Market price of Sunrise Ltd. shares: ₹18 per share
  • Expected volatility of shares: 35%
Questions:
  1. Calculate the debt and equity components of the compound instrument
  2. Prepare journal entries for initial recognition and first year interest
  3. Compute the effective interest rate and amortization schedule
  4. Explain the accounting treatment if conversion option is exercised

Case Study 5: GST Audit Discrepancies

Taxation GST Audit

During GST audit of M/s. ABC Enterprises for FY 2022-23, following discrepancies were noted:

Issue Amount (₹) Remarks
ITC claimed on invoices not reflected in GSTR-2A 12,75,000 Suppliers not filed returns
RCM not paid on legal services 3,20,000 From foreign law firm
Output tax short-paid due to wrong classification 8,45,000 18% instead of 28%

Additional information:

  • ABC Enterprises is registered as normal taxpayer
  • Annual turnover: ₹14.8 crores
  • All transactions are within India except legal services
Questions:
  1. Compute the total tax liability including interest and penalty
  2. Suggest corrective actions for each discrepancy
  3. Prepare disclosure notes for financial statements
  4. Explain the implications if these issues are identified during departmental audit

Case Study 6: Forensic Audit - Inventory Fraud

Auditing Forensic Accounting

You are appointed as forensic auditor for XYZ Manufacturing Ltd. following whistleblower complaints about inventory manipulation. Preliminary findings:

  • Physical inventory count shows ₹12.8 crores vs book value ₹15.2 crores
  • Unusual pattern of inventory movements between locations
  • Several GRNs without corresponding purchase orders
  • Discrepancies in scrap sales records

Additional information:

  • Company uses ERP system with inventory modules
  • Three warehouse locations across different states
  • Inventory manager joined 8 months back
Questions:
  1. Design audit procedures to investigate potential fraud
  2. Identify possible red flags in inventory management
  3. Suggest internal control improvements
  4. Explain reporting requirements under Companies Act

Case Study 7: Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process

Corporate Law IBC

ABC Ltd. (corporate debtor) owes:

  • Financial creditors: ₹120 crores (including ₹40 crores secured)
  • Operational creditors: ₹35 crores
  • Workmen dues: ₹8 crores

NCLT admitted insolvency application filed by a financial creditor. Resolution professional submitted following information:

  • Liquidation value: ₹90 crores
  • Going concern value: ₹110 crores
  • Resolution plan offers ₹105 crores
Questions:
  1. Explain the voting requirements for plan approval
  2. Calculate distribution as per waterfall mechanism
  3. Analyze tax implications of resolution plan
  4. Discuss treatment of contingent liabilities

Case Study 8: Revenue Recognition - Multiple Element Arrangement

Advanced Accounting Ind AS 115

TechSolutions Ltd. enters into following contracts:

Contract A: Software license (₹50 lakhs) + 3 years maintenance (₹15 lakhs/year) + customization (₹20 lakhs)

Contract B: Cloud hosting (₹8 lakhs/month) + implementation (one-time ₹25 lakhs)

Additional information:

  • Standalone selling prices not readily available
  • Customization services are typically sold separately for ₹15-25 lakhs
  • Implementation services completed in 3 months
Questions:
  1. Identify performance obligations in each contract
  2. Determine transaction price allocation
  3. Prepare revenue recognition schedule
  4. Explain disclosure requirements

Case Study 9: Transfer Pricing Audit

Taxation Transfer Pricing

IndianCo (manufacturer) sells products to ForeignCo (AE) at ₹1,200 per unit. Tax officer proposes adjustment to ₹1,500 based on:

Method Arm's Length Price (₹)
CUP (Company A) 1,550
CUP (Company B) 1,450
TNMM (OP/Sales 8%) 1,480

Additional information:

  • IndianCo's operating margin: 5%
  • Quantity discount of 5% given to AE
  • Credit period to AE: 90 days vs 30 days to others
Questions:
  1. Evaluate the transfer pricing methods used
  2. Compute possible adjustment and tax impact
  3. Suggest defense strategies for IndianCo
  4. Explain documentation requirements

Case Study 10: Lease Accounting - Lessor Perspective

Advanced Accounting Ind AS 116

Lessor Ltd. enters into 5-year lease for equipment with following terms:

  • Fair value: ₹50 lakhs
  • Annual lease payments: ₹12 lakhs (year-end)
  • Guaranteed residual value: ₹5 lakhs
  • Unguaranteed residual value: ₹3 lakhs
  • Implicit rate: 8% (known to lessee)

Additional information:

  • Initial direct costs: ₹1.2 lakhs
  • Equipment's useful life: 7 years
  • Lessor expects to recover all costs
Questions:
  1. Classify the lease as per Ind AS 116
  2. Prepare lease amortization schedule
  3. Compute net investment in lease
  4. Show accounting entries for first two years
